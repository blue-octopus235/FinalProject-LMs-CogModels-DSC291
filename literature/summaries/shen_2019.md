# Shen et al. (2019) — Ordered Neurons: Integrating Tree Structures into Recurrent Neural Networks

**Full citation:** Shen, Y., Tan, S., Sordoni, A., & Courville, A. (2019). Ordered neurons: Integrating tree structures into recurrent neural networks. In *Proceedings of ICLR 2019*.

**PDF:** https://arxiv.org/pdf/1810.09536.pdf  
**arXiv:** https://arxiv.org/abs/1810.09536  
**OpenReview:** https://openreview.net/forum?id=B1l6qiR5F7

## What the paper does

Introduces **ON-LSTM (Ordered Neurons LSTM)**, a modified LSTM that imposes an ordering constraint on the hidden state: neurons are split into "master" gates that control higher-level, longer-range information and "slave" gates for local/lower-level information. This induces a soft hierarchical structure — neurons that update slowly encode long-range (structural) information; fast-updating neurons encode local lexical information.

Critically, ON-LSTM learns this hierarchical structure **without gold parse trees**, unlike RNNG. It discovers approximate tree structure as a byproduct of learning to predict words.

Results:
- Competitive language modeling perplexity with standard LSTM.
- Unsupervised constituency parsing: trees induced from ON-LSTM hidden states correlate with gold parses better than standard LSTMs.
- Improved performance on targeted syntactic evaluations, especially long-distance dependencies.

## Relevance to our project

- **Fallback architecture**: Our methods draft explicitly names ON-LSTM as the fallback if RNNG training is infeasible within the compute budget. ON-LSTM provides a structural inductive bias without requiring parse supervision, making it significantly easier to train.
- **Intermediate position**: ON-LSTM sits between the plain LSTM (no structural bias) and RNNG (hard structural bias with gold trees). If we use it, we should frame it as a softer version of the structural bias, acknowledging it as a weaker test of the hierarchical-vs-sequential hypothesis.
- **Cite if used**: "If RNNG training is infeasible, we substitute ON-LSTM (Shen et al. 2019), a model with a soft structural inductive bias that does not require parse supervision."

## Key terms

- **Ordered neurons**: the key inductive constraint — a total ordering of neurons by update frequency, biasing slower neurons toward hierarchical information
- **Master/slave gates**: the specific gating mechanism implementing the ordering
- **Unsupervised parsing**: the ability to recover approximate tree structure without explicit parse supervision
